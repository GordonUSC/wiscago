Uncle G's
Field Notes.

Ten minutes of things that turn a great weekend into the best one of your life. Specifics you can deploy, the moments that matter, and the one discipline that makes all of it land. You already know how to love these people. This is just the cheat sheet.

The one rule Palmer House Pokémon Minecraft Northalsted The parade The boys Eric's day
01The one rule

Be the most present person in the room.

Everything below is tactics. This is the strategy. You are not the planner this weekend, and you are not the photographer. You are the uncle who is all the way here. The single highest-leverage move you can make, over and over: take one photo, then put the phone in your pocket. Kids and best friends can both tell the difference between someone recording a moment and someone living in it. Be the second one and the whole weekend tilts.

Show up. Put the phone away. Let the joy do the rest.
02The Palmer House

Your lobby is a museum. Use it.

Before you even drop bags, you can give the boys a jaw-drop. Three things to walk them to:

🦚

The Peacock Doors

Solid bronze, designed by Louis Comfort Tiffany, two peacocks facing each other. Each door weighs more than half a ton and the set is valued over a million dollars. They came from a jewelry store that lived inside the hotel. Tell the boys to find both peacocks' eyes.

🎨

Look straight up

The lobby ceiling holds 21 paintings of Greek myths (Aphrodite, Apollo, Pluto) by the French artist Louis Pierre Rigal. It was restored in 1996 by one of the people who restored the Sistine Chapel. Game for the boys: each finds one god nobody else spots.

🍫

The original brownie

Invented right here in 1893 for the World's Fair, on Bertha Palmer's orders. Get the real one at Lockwood, the hotel restaurant: walnuts, apricot glaze, the 130-year-old recipe. A late-night brownie with the boys is a memory for free.

Two extras if there is downtime or a tired afternoon:

  • The history tour (about 60 minutes) covers the brownie's birthplace, the Bertha and Potter romance, and a little museum of 100-year-old artifacts. It ends with a brownie. Worth it on a low-key morning.
  • The ghost stories. The Palmer House is famously "haunted" (flickering lights, phantom footsteps from the Great Chicago Fire era). For an 8 and 10 year old this is the perfect amount of spooky. Tell it at night, in the lobby, with the lights low.
03Saturday morning · Pokémon Fossil Museum

Make the boys feel like scientists.

The whole exhibit is built on one beautiful idea: a real fossil next to the Pokémon it inspired. Your job is to be the guide who makes them notice. The two showstoppers:

🦖

SUE meets Tyrantrum

A scientific cast of SUE the T. rex stands across from Tyrantrum, the T. rex Pokémon. This is the big one. Get the boys standing between them and ask: what did the artists copy, and what did they invent?

🪶

Archaeopteryx meets Archeops

Farther down, the Field's own famous Archaeopteryx (the feathered dino that bridges reptiles and birds) sits with Archeops. Quiet flex: this real fossil is one of the rarest objects in the building.

Grab this: ticket holders get a free Archeops promo card at the exhibit, while supplies last. Get one per boy. Instant treasure.
If anyone has Pokémon GO: the museum is geofenced during hours, with in-game Excavator Pikachu and Mega Aerodactyl raids. Open the app at the door.

Don't miss the real fossil-prep tools and the soundscape. And SUE is in the same building either way, so the visit is also just a great dinosaur day.

04Saturday afternoon · Minecraft Villager Rescue

Give every boy a job before you walk in.

This is a roughly one-hour, walk-through quest across eight rooms. A village is under zombie siege; you gather ingredients, craft a life-saving potion, and rescue the villagers. Here is how to make it sing:

  • Everyone gets an Orb of Interaction, a glowing handheld that drives the mission. The boys will know exactly what to do. You play too, fully in.
  • Assign roles up front, then rotate: one reads the clue, one gathers, one crafts. Switch every room. (This is literally one of the Arcade Team Quests, so they have practiced it.)
  • Dress for motion: climbing, jumping, exploring. Sneakers, not sandals.
  • The reward: a limited-edition half-zombie, half-villager cape for each guest at the end. Hype it on the way in so the finish line glows.
The single most important 30 minutes of Saturday

Between the museum and Minecraft, build in a real snack-and-water stop. Three kids, two giant experiences in one day: the meltdown always lives in the transition, not the events. A quiet half hour and a juice box saves the whole afternoon. This is the producer's move.

05Saturday evening · Northalsted

Walk the Legacy Walk.

Dinner in Northalsted, the city's historic LGBTQ+ neighborhood, and then a stroll the boys will not forget. The Legacy Walk runs along Halsted from Belmont to Grace: it is the world's only outdoor LGBTQ history museum, a row of rainbow pylons each honoring a hero. The rainbow crosswalks and pylons make it feel like the street itself is celebrating.

This is a soft, joyful primer for Sunday. Let the boys read a pylon or two. You are quietly teaching them that the parade tomorrow stands on real people. Keep dinner easy and casual; everyone needs legs for tomorrow.

06Sunday · The 55th Chicago Pride Parade

Where you stand decides the day.

Theme: Free to Be Proud. Steps off 11 AM at Sheridan and Broadway. Here is the insider geography that matters with three kids:

📍

Stand here, not there

The crush is on Halsted between Belmont and Addison. With kids, pick a less-packed stretch: Broadway between Belmont and Oakdale, or Belmont between Halsted and Broadway. Same parade, room to breathe, sightlines for short people.

🚇

Get there clean

Red Line to Belmont or Addison beats any car. Aim to be on the curb by 10:15. Front rows go first, and three boys need front rows.

🤝

The meeting-spot rule

Before step-off, name one fixed landmark out loud to every kid as the spot to go if anyone gets separated. Say it twice. Then relax all day.

Point the boys to the grand marshals when they roll by: Tracy Baim (founded Windy City Times), Mona Noriega and Evette Cardona (decades of activism), and the Alliance of Illinois Judges led by Judge Jill Rose Quinn, Illinois's first openly trans judge. Floats throw candy and beads; tell the boys to face forward, hands visible. Pack water, hats, sunscreen.

07The boys' cheat sheet

James, Henry, Oscar.

James is 10 and the oldest. Henry and Oscar are 8-year-old twins (Henry is red and green, Oscar is orange and blue, on every page of their Arcade for a reason). A few keys that make you the cool uncle on tap:

  • Pokémon flex: a fossil Pokémon is one you revive from a fossil. Tyrantrum comes from the Jaw Fossil, Aerodactyl from Old Amber. Drop that at the museum and watch their faces.
  • Minecraft flex: the cure for a zombie villager really is a Splash Potion of Weakness plus a golden apple. You know the recipe. Be the one who knows.
  • Norwegian, because Stoughton: Hei (hi), Takk (thanks), Tusen takk (a thousand thanks), Venn (friend). Teach them one word and have them teach it back.
  • The Arcade is loaded at the link below. It keeps one shared Team Trophy, so they win together, not against each other. Hand it over in any line or car ride.
08Eric's day

This is the one that matters most.

Sunday is Eric's first Pride. He has built the rooms where this whole community gathers, and he has never once stood on the route as a participant. You get to give him that. The craft of it is small and quiet:

  • Find the curb, get the boys up front, then step back and let him just be in it. Don't narrate it. Don't manage it. Stand beside him and let it wash over.
  • One question, early: ask him what he is feeling, then actually listen to the whole answer. You have built a life out of listening. Do it here.
  • The photo discipline applies double. One picture of him on the route, phone away, eyes up. He will remember your face next to his, not your screen.
The boys will remember the floats. The grown-ups will remember this.

After Chicago it is Wisconsin, the Dragon House, the Fourth of July. But Sunday on that curb, next to your friend, watching him discover he was always free to be proud: that is the best weekend of your life, right there. Go get it.
