Who Gordon is

CEO of a community. Brother by choice.

CEO of Gay Gaming Professionals, a 501(c)(3) twenty years in service. Three years in partnership with Wizards of the Coast at Zero Proof Board Game Night. A teaching post at USC. A speaker who travels for the work and a friend who travels for the people.

CEO of GGP

Gay Gaming Professionals, the 501(c)(3) Gordon leads. Universal excellence, particular lens. The lens positions GGP to serve and center underestimated communities. Not niche.

Eric's brother

Not by birth. By choice and by years. Eric is the one who built the table; Gordon is the one who travels to sit at it. They are family.

Brother to the family

James, Henry, and Oscar know him as their dad's chosen brother. The visitor with stories from gaming conferences, a guitar in his voice, and a hug ready before anyone asks.

What Gordon brings to Chicago

Show up. Be there. Let it be enough.

Free to be proud. Free to be present. Free to be family. The arrival mood

For Chicago, Gordon brings what he always brings. Curiosity for the museum. Patience for the boys. Energy for the parade. Love for Crystal and Eric. And then a ride to Wisconsin after Chicago, because the trip does not end at the Pride parade. It ends at the Dragon House, with everyone home.

For the trip

Gordon's job: be Uncle G. Bring snacks. Carry someone if they get tired. Take the picture, then put the phone away.
