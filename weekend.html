Home base · The Loop

The Palmer House.

We are staying at the Palmer House, a Hilton Hotel, at 17 East Monroe Street in the heart of the Loop. One of the longest continuously operating hotels in the country, and it has a story the boys are going to love.

🍫

The brownie was born here

In 1893, Bertha Palmer asked the hotel's pastry chef for a treat that would fit in a boxed lunch for the World's Columbian Exposition. The result was the very first brownie: dense and fudgy, with a walnut and apricot glaze. They still make the original recipe. Yes, you can order one.

🎨

Look up in the lobby

The lobby ceiling is a hand-painted French fresco. Bertha Palmer, who befriended Claude Monet in Paris, filled the place with Impressionist art. The grand staircase and the Tiffany details are worth a slow walk.

🏛️

A hotel of firsts

The first fireproof hotel and one of the first anywhere to use the electric lightbulb. Opened in 1871 as Potter Palmer's wedding gift to Bertha, rebuilt after the Great Chicago Fire, and welcoming guests ever since.

📍

Right in the middle

Steps from Millennium Park and Cloud Gate (the Bean), the Art Institute, and the Riverwalk. An easy base for the Field Museum on Saturday and a short Red Line ride toward the parade on Sunday.

Palmer House, a Hilton Hotel · 17 E Monroe St, Chicago, IL 60603 · Hilton Honors applies. Tip for the boys: each carries a card with the hotel name and address.

Saturday June 27

The family adventure day.

Two of the biggest pop-culture experiences in the country, both in Chicago this summer, both happening for us in one Saturday.

Morning · Field Museum

Pokémon Fossil Museum. The first time this exhibition has traveled outside Japan. Life-size models of fossil Pokémon (Tyrantrum, Archeops, more) displayed alongside real fossils from the Field's collection, including SUE the T. rex. May 22 2026 through April 11 2027.

Tickets

Weekday: $18 adult, $13 kid. Weekend: $20 adult, $14 kid. (Saturday is a weekend day.)

What to see first

SUE the T. rex is in the same building. Make sure the boys get to stand under her.

Programming

The Field is running paleontology activities tied to the exhibit. Check the day-of schedule.

Afternoon · Rosemont

Minecraft Experience: Villager Rescue. An immersive, walk-through Minecraft adventure. Eight interactive rooms, about 55 minutes inside. You and the family gather ingredients, craft a life-saving potion, and rescue the villagers from a zombie attack. All ages welcome.

Where

EXP Rosemont, 9900 Berwyn Ave, Rosemont, IL 60018. Free parking on site.

Tickets

Starting at $28. Multi-pack and group options available.

After the rescue

Trading Post with themed souvenirs. Unlock a commemorative in-game cape with admission.

Evening: dinner in Northalsted (the city's historic LGBTQ+ neighborhood). Easy stroll along the Legacy Walk before settling in for the night.

Sunday June 28

The 55th annual Chicago Pride Parade.

Theme: Free to Be Proud. Stepping off 11 AM at Sheridan and Broadway. Two miles through Northalsted.

The route

South on Broadway → south on Halsted → east on Belmont → south on Broadway → east on Diversey to Cannon Drive. Picks up the energy through the heart of the LGBTQ+ corridor and ends at the edge of Lincoln Park.

Tracy Baim

Grand Marshal. Founder of Windy City Times. Decades documenting LGBTQ+ Chicago.

Mona Noriega

Grand Marshal. Opened Lambda Legal Midwest. On the board of Equality Illinois.

Evette Cardona

Grand Marshal. Long-time activist alongside Mona Noriega.

Alliance of Illinois Judges

Grand Marshal. Led by Judge Jill Rose Quinn, Illinois's first openly trans judge and elected official.

For the boys: bring water, hats, sunscreen, and your loudest cheer. For everyone: bring the heart.

First-timer field guide · Eric's first Pride

The parade is a two-mile-long front porch. Everyone is already rooting for you. A few things that make it great with kids:

  • Get to Sheridan & Broadway by 10:15 AM — curb space fills fast, front rows go first, and three boys need front rows.
  • Pick a fixed meeting spot before step-off. A corner landmark (the Dunkin' on Broadway works). Say it out loud to every kid.
  • Floats throw candy and beads. Boys should face forward and keep hands visible.
  • The Energy peaks around the Halsted turn — that's the heart of it. If someone's legs are giving out, hold there.
  • Put the phone away after one photo. Be in it.
